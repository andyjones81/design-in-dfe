Be accessible from the start

Accessibility must never be an afterthought or a task to do once you've got MVP out the door.

Designers creating user interactions and writing content, must consider who may be using our service, or may do in the future.

Allowing our users to use services which are not accessible, makes the service harder to use, wastes time and money fixing problems in the future, and exposes us to unnecessary risk.

Design for less

Less is more. Creative use of whitespace can make things easier to understand, navigate and use.

Some things in design are functional, others purely aesthetic. If they aren't adding to the overall experience, then remove it - reduce the design to the necessary features for the user to complete their task.

When writing content, write only what you need to.
