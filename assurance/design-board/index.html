Design Board

The Design Board helps teams get advice and recommendations on design and accessibility problems and de-risk services in a safe environment.

People Time
Up to 4 people Up to 50 minutes

Why use a design board

You can make use of the accessibility and design board to:

  • get advice on a problem to do with any element of design or accessibility
  • get support with escalating a problem or risk
  • ask for feedback or recommendations or decisions on your design
  • take part in a light-touch audit and understanding any accessibility issues you may have
  • get help in putting together a brief for getting an accessibility audit

Prepare for a board

When you bring a problem to a board, come with the following questions:

What is the problem

Be able to articulate whats wrong and the evidence, or, if you’re stuck on a design, what are you trying to do.

Bring sketches, prototypes (HTML / Kit / Figma / Lucid etc).

What outcome are you looking for

Do you want a solution, advice on what to do, recommendations for next steps, ideas on how you can achieve your outcome?

What have you tried already

Talk about what you’ve considered, looked at, researched - and why some of these haven’t worked.

Timescales and constraints

What are your timescales for implementing a solution and what constraints are you working with?

Pressures

The board is a safe space to talk. If you are being pressured into a particular solution or outcome you don’t agree with, talk about this, where the pressure is coming from and why, this may be a problem with ways of working and how we expect people to behave in DfE.

Board follow up

The board aims to work out next steps based on the outcome you are after and the problem.

You’ll get an email after the board, usually within a few hours, confirming what’s been discussed and any actions.

If you have raised any concerns or pressures, we will not include these in the follow up email. This may be followed up separately if needed, or if there is a concern about ways of working.
